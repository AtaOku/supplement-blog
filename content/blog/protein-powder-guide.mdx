## What Is Protein Powder?

Protein powder is a dietary supplement that helps you meet your daily protein requirements. It's especially valuable for athletes, active individuals, and anyone struggling to get enough protein from whole foods alone.

## Types of Protein Powder

### Whey Protein

Whey is the most popular protein source, derived from milk. Its fast absorption makes it ideal for post-workout recovery.

- **Whey Concentrate**: 70-80% protein, affordable price
- **Whey Isolate**: 90%+ protein, low lactose
- **Whey Hydrolysate**: Pre-digested, fastest absorption

### Casein Protein

Casein digests slowly, providing a sustained release of amino acids over several hours. Ideal before bed to support overnight muscle recovery.

### Vegan Protein

Made from pea, rice, soy, or hemp sources. Suitable for those with lactose intolerance and vegans. Modern blends have significantly improved taste and texture.

## How Much Protein Do You Need?

| Activity Level | Daily Protein |
|---|---|
| Sedentary | 0.8g/kg |
| Lightly Active | 1.2g/kg |
| Athlete | 1.6-2.2g/kg |
| Muscle Building | 2.0-2.4g/kg |

## What to Look for When Choosing Protein Powder

1. **Protein content**: At least 20g per serving
2. **Ingredients**: Avoid unnecessary fillers and additives
3. **Amino acid profile**: Should contain all essential amino acids
4. **Taste and mixability**: Personal preference matters
5. **Cost per gram**: Calculate the protein cost per serving, not just the price tag

## The Bottom Line

Your protein powder choice depends on your goals, dietary habits, and budget. The most important rule: use it as a supplement when you can't meet your protein needs through whole foods alone. Real food first, powder second.
