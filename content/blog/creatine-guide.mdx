## What Is Creatine?

Creatine is an amino acid naturally produced by the body and found in foods like meat and fish. It plays a critical role in energy production within muscles — and increasingly, research shows it benefits the brain too.

## Why Take Creatine?

Based on extensive scientific research, creatine:

- Increases muscle strength and power output
- Supports muscle mass gains
- Improves performance in high-intensity exercise
- May benefit brain health and cognitive function
- Supports mitochondrial energy transport

Creatine is one of the most studied and evidence-backed supplements in existence.

## Types of Creatine

**Creatine monohydrate** is the most researched and most effective form. Other forms (HCL, buffered, ethyl ester) are more expensive but have not been proven to offer additional benefits.

Save your money — stick with monohydrate.

## Dosage and Usage

### Loading Phase (Optional)
- 20g daily, split into 4 doses, for 5-7 days

### Maintenance Dose
- 3-5g daily

The loading phase is not required. Taking 3-5g daily will fully saturate muscle creatine stores within 3-4 weeks.

## When Should You Take It?

Creatine is flexible when it comes to timing. It can be taken before or after training — or any time of day. Consistency matters more than timing.

## Side Effects

Creatine is generally very safe. The most common side effect is mild water retention, which actually contributes to increased muscle volume. Claims about kidney damage have been repeatedly debunked in healthy individuals.

## Beyond Muscle: Creatine for Mitochondria and Brain

Recent research highlights creatine's role beyond muscle:
- The creatine-phosphocreatine system distributes ATP from mitochondria throughout cells
- Neuroprotective effects have been demonstrated in research
- May support cognitive function, especially under stress or sleep deprivation

## The Bottom Line

Creatine monohydrate is one of the most scientifically supported supplements available. It's affordable, effective, and safe. If you exercise regularly, it's one of the few supplements that's almost universally recommended.
