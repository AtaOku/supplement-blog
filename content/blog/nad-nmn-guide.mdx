## What Is NAD+ and Why Does It Matter?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a critical coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It's involved in over 500 enzymatic reactions and manages these vital functions:

- **Mitochondrial energy production** (ATP synthesis)
- **DNA repair** (via PARP enzymes)
- **Sirtuin activation** (the "longevity genes")
- **Cellular signaling**
- **Circadian rhythm regulation**

### The NAD+ Decline: A Core Driver of Aging

NAD+ levels drop dramatically with age:
- Noticeable decline begins around age 40
- By age 60, NAD+ levels are roughly 50% of youthful levels
- This decline is directly linked to mitochondrial dysfunction, DNA damage accumulation, and cellular aging

Harvard's Dr. David Sinclair has described NAD+ decline as not just a consequence of aging, but a cause of it.

## How to Boost NAD+

Taking NAD+ directly as a supplement isn't effective because it gets broken down in the digestive system. Instead, NAD+ precursors are used:

### NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)
- Direct precursor in the NAD+ synthesis pathway
- Rapidly converted to NAD+ in the body
- The supplement David Sinclair personally uses

### NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)
- Converted to NMN first, then to NAD+ (indirect pathway)
- Smaller molecule, some researchers suggest better absorption
- Popularized by ChromaDex's Tru Niagen brand

### Other Methods
- **Exercise**: Naturally boosts NAD+ production
- **Calorie restriction / fasting**: Activates NAD+ pathways
- **Niacin (Vitamin B3)**: NAD+ precursor, but causes flushing side effects

## NMN vs NR: Which Is Better?

This is one of the most debated questions in the longevity community.

| Feature | NMN | NR |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Conversion to NAD+ | Direct | Indirect (via NMN) |
| Molecule size | Larger | Smaller |
| Clinical trial volume | Rapidly growing | More established |
| Bioavailability | Good (sublingual forms better) | Good |
| Sinclair's choice | NMN | — |
| ChromaDex patent | No | Yes (Tru Niagen) |
| Price | More expensive | More affordable |
| FDA status (2026) | Available as supplement | Available as supplement |

### Practical Advice
Both effectively raise NAD+ levels. The deciding factors:
- **Budget**: NR is generally more affordable
- **Research interest**: NMN is attracting more recent studies
- **Availability**: Both are available online worldwide

## NMN and FDA: The 2026 Situation

NMN was briefly classified by the FDA as an "investigational new drug," creating uncertainty about its legal status as a supplement. However, in January 2026, NMN was cleared to return to the supplement category. This decision triggered a major market resurgence.

## Scientific Evidence

### Animal Studies
- NMN supplementation rejuvenated mitochondrial function in aged mice
- Improvements in muscle strength, endurance, and metabolic parameters
- Enhanced cognitive function and memory
- Improved cardiac function

### Human Studies
- NMN supplementation safely raises NAD+ levels in humans (confirmed)
- Improved muscle insulin sensitivity in prediabetic individuals
- Enhanced aerobic capacity and physical performance
- Improved sleep quality in older adults

### Ongoing Research
As of 2026, numerous human clinical trials for NMN and NR are underway. Long-term safety and efficacy data is still accumulating.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is NAD+ supplementation safe?
Yes — clinical studies have found NMN and NR to be safe. However, long-term (10+ year) data is not yet available.

### When will I feel results?
Most users report increased energy within 2-4 weeks. Measurable NAD+ increases can be observed from the first week.

### What age should I start?
General recommendation: preventive doses from the late 30s. Higher doses can be considered after 40.

### Is it safe for cancer patients?
This is a debated topic. NAD+ could theoretically fuel both healthy and cancerous cells. If you have a cancer diagnosis, consult your oncologist before use.
